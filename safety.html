Safety

Warm Up

Always warm up before playing to reduce injury risk and improve your performance.

  • Stretch your legs, arms, and ankles
  • Do light jogging to get your heart rate up
  • Move your body gently before stronger drills

Protection Gear

Wear proper protective equipment to stay safe while you play.

  • Use ankle supports if needed
  • Wear knee pads for extra protection
  • Choose good basketball shoes with support

Rest

Rest is part of safe training. Listen to your body and do not overdo it.

  • Do not play too long without a break
  • Drink water and stay hydrated
  • Stop if you feel pain or tiredness

Footwear

Choosing the right shoes keeps you stable and prevents slips on the court.

  • Wear shoes made for basketball
  • Avoid playing in slippers
  • Replace worn-out shoes regularly

Stretching

Stretch after practice to keep muscles flexible and reduce soreness.

  • Stretch gently, without bouncing
  • Hold each stretch for 15 seconds
  • Focus on legs and arms after practice

Injury Care

Take care of small injuries quickly so they do not become bigger problems.

  • Check your body for soreness
  • Use ice if a joint feels swollen
  • See a doctor if pain does not go away
